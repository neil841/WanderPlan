# User Personas - WanderPlan

> Created by Product Strategy Advisor on 2025-11-08

These personas represent the primary user segments for WanderPlan. Each persona has distinct goals, pain points, and feature priorities.

---

## Persona 1: Sarah Mitchell - "The Solo Adventurer"

![Persona Type: Individual Traveler]

### Demographics
- **Age**: 28
- **Occupation**: Marketing Manager at tech startup
- **Location**: San Francisco, CA
- **Tech Level**: Advanced (daily app user, comfortable with cloud tools)
- **Income**: $85K/year
- **Travel Frequency**: 3-4 international trips per year

### Background
Sarah is a millennial professional who values experiences over possessions. She works remotely 2 days/week, allowing her to take extended trips. She's detail-oriented and likes to plan thoroughly but stay flexible during trips.

### Goals
- **Primary**: Create detailed itineraries for solo trips to feel organized and safe
- **Secondary**: Share trip plans with family for safety
- **Aspirational**: Document trips beautifully to share with friends on social media

### Pain Points
- **Current Problem 1**: Uses Google Docs for itineraries - messy, no map integration
- **Current Problem 2**: Hard to visualize day-by-day timeline
- **Current Problem 3**: Forgets to share plans with emergency contacts
- **Current Problem 4**: Difficult to track expenses across multiple currencies

### How She'll Use WanderPlan
- Creates new trip 2 months before departure
- Researches and adds activities daily over 6-8 weeks
- Uses map view to check if itinerary makes geographic sense
- Shares read-only link with parents for safety
- Updates itinerary on-the-go from mobile
- Tracks expenses to stay within budget ($2-3K per trip)
- Exports PDF before trip for offline access

### Must-Have Features (from her perspective)
1. **Drag-and-drop itinerary builder** - Visual organization is key
2. **Interactive maps** - Needs to see where everything is
3. **Mobile-responsive design** - Plans from phone during lunch breaks
4. **PDF export** - Wants offline backup
5. **Budget tracking** - Needs to stay within limits
6. **Trip sharing** - Parents want to know where she is

### Nice-to-Have Features
- Weather integration (helps with packing)
- Trip cover images (aesthetic preference)
- Dark mode (works late at night planning)

### Quote
> "I love traveling solo, but I need to stay organized. A messy itinerary stresses me out and wastes precious vacation time."

### User Journey
1. **Discovery**: Finds WanderPlan via travel blog recommendation
2. **Onboarding**: Creates account, sees empty state with tutorial
3. **First Trip**: Plans 2-week Japan trip with 15 activities
4. **Value Moment**: Realizes map view shows she planned Tokyo poorly (too much back-and-forth)
5. **Retention**: Now plans all trips in WanderPlan, shares with friends

### Success Metrics
- Creates 3-4 trips per year
- Adds 10-15 events per trip
- Shares trip with 2-3 people
- Exports PDF for each trip
- Mobile usage: 40% of interactions

## Persona 2: Mike Chen - "The Group Coordinator"

![Persona Type: Group Trip Organizer]

### Demographics
- **Age**: 35
- **Occupation**: Software Engineer at Fortune 500 company
- **Location**: Austin, TX
- **Tech Level**: Expert (works in tech, power user)
- **Family**: Married with 2 kids (ages 5, 7)
- **Travel Frequency**: 2 family vacations + 1 reunion per year

### Background
Mike is the "tech guy" in his extended family, so he's always asked to organize group trips. He coordinates annual family reunions (15-20 people) and quarterly trips with his wife and kids. He values efficiency and hates repetitive tasks.

### Goals
- **Primary**: Coordinate group trips without endless email chains
- **Secondary**: Get input from family members without chaos
- **Tertiary**: Split costs fairly among participants

### Pain Points
- **Current Problem 1**: Email threads with 30+ messages become unmanageable
- **Current Problem 2**: Hard to get consensus on restaurant choices
- **Current Problem 3**: Manually tracking who paid for what is tedious
- **Current Problem 4**: People forget agreed-upon plans
- **Current Problem 5**: Can't reuse last year's itinerary easily

### How He'll Use WanderPlan
- Creates trip, immediately invites family members as editors
- Uses polls feature to decide on restaurants and activities
- Assigns tasks (e.g., "Sarah, book the Airbnb")
- Tracks who paid for group meals
- Uses expense splitting to calculate settlements
- Duplicates previous year's reunion trip as starting point
- Shares final itinerary with everyone (including grandparents who don't edit)

### Must-Have Features (from his perspective)
1. **Collaboration with permissions** - Needs viewer/editor roles
2. **Real-time messaging** - Avoid email
3. **Polls & ideas** - Democratic decision-making
4. **Expense splitting** - Fair cost allocation
5. **Trip sharing** - Easy access for everyone
6. **Trip duplication** - Reuse templates

### Nice-to-Have Features
- Activity feed (see who changed what)
- Bulk operations (archive old trips)
- Advanced filters (find past Disney trips)

### Quote
> "Organizing a family reunion shouldn't feel like a part-time job. I need tools that help us decide together and keep everyone on the same page."

### User Journey
1. **Discovery**: Recommended by colleague who used it for bachelor party
2. **Onboarding**: Creates account, creates reunion trip, invites 15 family members
3. **Collaboration**: Uses polls to decide on 3 dinner restaurants
4. **Value Moment**: Expense splitting automatically calculates who owes whom
5. **Retention**: Uses for all future family trips, became family's "travel planning platform"

### Success Metrics
- Creates 3 trips per year (2 family, 1 reunion)
- Invites average 8 collaborators per trip
- 25+ messages exchanged per trip
- 3-5 polls created per trip
- Duplicates previous trips 50% of the time

## Persona 3: Jessica Rodriguez - "The Professional Agent"

![Persona Type: Travel Agent / Professional]

### Demographics
- **Age**: 42
- **Occupation**: Independent Travel Consultant (10 years experience)
- **Location**: Miami, FL
- **Tech Level**: Intermediate (comfortable with CRM tools, less with technical tools)
- **Business**: Solo operation, 20-30 active clients at any time
- **Revenue**: $120K/year from commissions and planning fees

### Background
Jessica left a corporate travel agency 3 years ago to start her own consultancy. She specializes in luxury European vacations and destination weddings. She needs professional tools but can't afford $300/month enterprise software. Most of her clients are high-net-worth individuals who expect white-glove service.

### Goals
- **Primary**: Create professional itineraries that wow clients
- **Secondary**: Manage client relationships and trip history
- **Tertiary**: Invoice clients for planning fees
- **Growth**: Attract new clients through referrals

### Pain Points
- **Current Problem 1**: Travefy is too expensive ($50/month + add-ons)
- **Current Problem 2**: Manually creating invoices in QuickBooks
- **Current Problem 3**: Client data scattered across email, spreadsheets, and notes
- **Current Problem 4**: Hard to showcase past work to prospects
- **Current Problem 5**: Clients lose printed itineraries

### How She'll Use WanderPlan
- Creates detailed itineraries for each client trip (20-30 events)
- Adds luxury hotels, Michelin restaurants, exclusive experiences
- Uploads booking confirmations and travel documents
- Shares branded itinerary (with her logo) with clients
- Tracks client preferences (vegetarian, hates early mornings, etc.)
- Generates professional invoices for $500-2000 planning fees
- Uses website builder to create landing page for new client acquisition
- Sends payment link via Stripe

### Must-Have Features (from her perspective)
1. **CRM for client profiles** - Store preferences and history
2. **Professional proposals** - Impress high-end clients
3. **Invoicing with Stripe** - Get paid easily
4. **Document management** - Store all booking confirmations
5. **Customizable branding** - White-label experience
6. **Website builder** - Marketing and lead capture
7. **PDF export** - Clients want beautiful printouts

### Nice-to-Have Features
- Lead capture forms (integrated into website)
- Email templates (automated follow-ups)
- Trip templates (similar clients often want similar trips)

### Quote
> "My clients expect perfection. I need tools that make me look professional without charging me enterprise prices."

### User Journey
1. **Discovery**: Searches "Travefy alternative free" on Google
2. **Onboarding**: Creates account, explores business features
3. **First Client**: Creates luxury Italy itinerary for couple
4. **Value Moment**: Generates professional invoice, receives payment via Stripe within 24 hours
5. **Scaling**: Migrates all 20 active clients to WanderPlan, cancels Travefy
6. **Growth**: Builds landing page, gets 3 new clients from website

### Success Metrics
- Creates 20-25 trips per year
- Manages 20-30 client profiles
- Sends 20-25 invoices per year ($25K+ total)
- Average 25 events per trip
- 80% of clients are repeat/referral

### Revenue Potential for WanderPlan
- Willing to pay $20-30/month for Pro plan
- Needs unlimited trips, clients, and advanced features
- High lifetime value customer (sticky due to CRM data)

## Persona 4: David Park - "The Travel Enthusiast"

![Persona Type: Travel Blogger / Enthusiast]

### Demographics
- **Age**: 50
- **Occupation**: Retired High School Teacher
- **Location**: Portland, OR
- **Tech Level**: Intermediate (comfortable with apps, not a programmer)
- **Travel Frequency**: 6-8 trips per year (short and long)
- **Hobbies**: Travel blogging, photography

### Background
David retired early and now travels full-time. He writes a travel blog with 5,000 monthly readers and creates detailed destination guides. He loves discovering hidden gems and sharing them. He's passionate about sustainable travel and budget optimization.

### Goals
- **Primary**: Create comprehensive destination guides to share on blog
- **Secondary**: Inspire others to travel
- **Tertiary**: Collaborate with other bloggers on group trips

### Pain Points
- **Current Problem 1**: Blog posts don't include interactive maps
- **Current Problem 2**: Readers ask "can I copy your itinerary?"
- **Current Problem 3**: Hard to share detailed plans with fellow bloggers
- **Current Problem 4**: No good way to crowdsource local recommendations

### How He'll Use WanderPlan
- Creates "ultimate" destination guides (e.g., "7 Days in Vietnam - $500 Budget")
- Makes trips public so blog readers can duplicate them
- Adds detailed notes to every activity (insider tips)
- Collaborates with local guides to add hidden gems
- Exports trip as shareable link embedded in blog posts
- Encourages readers to duplicate and customize his itineraries
- Uses tags to organize trips by country, budget level, type (adventure/luxury/budget)

### Must-Have Features (from his perspective)
1. **Public trip sharing** - Readers need easy access
2. **Trip duplication** - Let readers copy his itineraries
3. **Detailed event notes** - Share insider knowledge
4. **Interactive maps** - Visual storytelling
5. **Search & tags** - Organize 50+ trips
6. **PDF export** - Readers want printable guides

### Nice-to-Have Features
- Trip cover images (aesthetic blog integration)
- Social sharing (amplify reach)
- Import from other apps (migrate old Google Maps lists)
- Statistics dashboard (see most popular trips)

### Quote
> "I've spent years discovering the best spots in 30+ countries. I want to share this knowledge in a way that's actually useful, not just a blog post people skim and forget."

### User Journey
1. **Discovery**: Sees another travel blogger using it
2. **Onboarding**: Creates account, migrates 10 favorite itineraries
3. **First Share**: Embeds Thailand trip in blog post
4. **Value Moment**: Gets feedback from reader who duplicated trip
5. **Advocacy**: Becomes power user, mentions WanderPlan in every post

### Success Metrics
- Creates 30-50 trips (comprehensive guides)
- Each trip public and shareable
- Average 500+ views per popular trip
- Other users duplicate his trips 100+ times
- Active in community (if forums exist)

## Design Implications

### For Sarah (Solo Traveler)
- **UX Focus**: Simple, clean interface. No clutter.
- **Mobile First**: 40% of usage is mobile.
- **Speed**: Fast loading, instant feedback.

### For Mike (Group Coordinator)
- **Collaboration Tools**: Real-time updates, presence indicators.
- **Notification Management**: Don't overwhelm with alerts.
- **Efficiency**: Keyboard shortcuts, bulk operations.

### For Jessica (Professional Agent)
- **Professional Polish**: Premium look and feel.
- **Branding Options**: Custom colors, logo placement.
- **Reliability**: Can't have downtime during client meetings.
- **Support**: Needs responsive customer support.

### For David (Enthusiast)
- **Public Profiles**: Make sharing seamless.
- **Content Tools**: Rich text for notes, galleries.
- **Discovery**: Help readers find great itineraries.

---

## Recommended MVP Priority

**Phase 1 (MVP)**: Focus on Sarah and Mike
- Individual travelers (Sarah) are the largest market segment
- Group coordinators (Mike) provide viral growth (invite friends/family)
- Both can use free tier forever (sustainable for platform)

**Phase 2**: Add David's features
- Public sharing and duplication enable content creation
- Drives SEO and organic growth
- Still mostly free tier users

**Phase 3-4**: Add Jessica's business features
- CRM, invoicing, payments are monetization features
- Target professionals willing to pay $20-30/month
- This funds platform development for free users

---

These personas will guide all design and development decisions. Each feature should be evaluated: "Does this serve Sarah, Mike, Jessica, or David? How?"
